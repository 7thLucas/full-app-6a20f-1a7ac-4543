# GlowDesk — Product

## What it is
GlowDesk is a mobile-first salon management app for small-to-medium beauty salon owners (1–10 stylists). It replaces WhatsApp threads, paper appointment books, and scattered spreadsheets with one platform centered on smart appointment booking, stylist management, product inventory tracking, and client retention.

## Users

**Primary: The Hands-On Salon Owner / Manager**
- Owns or manages a salon with 1–10 stylists; often works as a stylist themselves
- Phone and tablet are the primary work surfaces — uses the app between clients
- Currently runs ops on WhatsApp, paper books, or basic spreadsheets
- Motivated by revenue growth and client loyalty, not technology for its own sake
- Not technical; no patience for complex onboarding or dense dashboards
- Needs tools that are fast to open, obvious to use, actionable at a glance

**Secondary: The Stylist**
- Executes appointments and handles day-to-day client interactions
- Needs clear visibility into their own schedule and assigned services
- Benefits from client history notes and product availability information

## Brand

- **Name:** GlowDesk
- **Tagline direction:** "Every chair, every client, every glow."
- **Tone:** Confident, warm, practical. Direct without being cold. Premium without being inaccessible. Replace chaos, not habits — mirror how salons already think and speak.
- **Voice rules:**
  - Speak in salon-native language: "chair," "client," "stylist," "service," "no-show," "walk-in"
  - Action-first verbs in UI: "Book," "Remind," "Restock," "Follow up"
  - Never use sterile enterprise-speak ("entity," "record," "submit form")
  - Numbers and outcomes over feature names — "3 hours saved this week," not "automation engine active"

## Anti-references
- **Not** a sterile enterprise SaaS dashboard (Salesforce, SAP, generic admin panels)
- **Not** a consumer beauty marketplace (Treatwell, Booksy from the client perspective) — GlowDesk is the operator's tool
- **Not** a generic calendar app (Google Calendar, Calendly) — it is salon-specific, with stylist/service/inventory context
- **Not** a clinical health-tech aesthetic (blue/white hospital UI)
- **Not** a Web3 / fintech bro aesthetic (dark mode neon, aggressive gradients)

## Strategic Principles
1. **Mobile-first, always.** Every interaction must be touch-native and survive one-handed use between clients.
2. **Replace chaos, not habits.** Mirror how salons already think — language, flows, and outputs feel familiar from day one.
3. **Revenue over features.** Every screen is justified by bookings won, no-shows prevented, products saved, or clients retained.
4. **Calm, not clinical.** Warm, polished, premium — the product feels like the salon itself, not the IT department.
5. **One-glance answers.** The owner should learn the state of the salon in under 3 seconds per screen.

## MVP Scope — Smart Appointment Booking
The highest-leverage problem: missed and mismanaged bookings.

- **Real-time stylist availability calendar** — visual, conflict-free scheduling across all stylists; no double-bookings possible
- **Client self-booking link** — shareable booking link; clients choose stylist, service, and time slot
- **Automated reminders** — SMS/push reminders before appointments to reduce no-shows
- **Appointment management** — owner can view, create, reschedule, and cancel appointments from mobile

## Additional Core Scope (in priority order)
1. **Stylist management** — schedules, specialties, performance tracking
2. **Product inventory tracking** — real-time stock levels, low-stock alerts, usage per service
3. **Client retention loop** — post-visit follow-ups, loyalty rewards, visit history, re-engagement nudges for lapsed clients (60+ days)

## Out of Scope (MVP)
- Multi-location chains (11+ stylists, franchises)
- Point-of-sale / payment processing
- Payroll & HR
- Social media / marketing campaign tools
- Consumer-facing marketplace

## Success Metrics
- Booking fill rate: +25% in 60 days
- No-show rate: under 10%
- Admin time saved: 6+ hours/week per owner
- Client return rate within 90 days: +20 percentage points
- Inventory stockout incidents: zero
